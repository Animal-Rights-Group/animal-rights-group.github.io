Welcome to our Website

Our Mission

The goal of Animal Rights International is to raise awareness about our cruel treatment of animals in animal industries. We want to draw attention to the conditions in such industries and, through education and awareness, reduce the breeding, mistreatment and killing of animals for food, clothing, laboratory experiments and entertainment in the long term, replacing them with animal-free alternatives.

Our Approach

With our articles, we want to offer everyone the opportunity to engage with these very important topics. That is why our articles are primarily aimed at readers who are not yet familiar with these issues. We try to explain everything as simply as possible and from the ground up so that everyone can follow the explanations. For those who are already familiar with these topics and want to delve deeper into them, we also have something to offer: an extensive collection of organisations, projects, social media channels, scientific papers and much more on these topics.

Our Topics

Veganism

Veganism is a complex and, for many people, a very emotional topic. Unlike vegetarianism, veganism is not just a diet, but extends to all areas of life. There are different definitions of the term veganism, but the most commonly cited and widely used is that of the Vegan Society, which defines veganism as "a philosophy and way of living which seeks to exclude — as far as is possible and practicable — all forms of exploitation of, and cruelty to, animals for food, clothing or any other purpose; and by extension, promotes the development and use of animal-free alternatives for the benefit of animals, humans and the environment. In dietary terms, it denotes the practice of dispensing with all products derived wholly or partly from animals". read more

Animal Rights

It has been scientifically proven that, in addition to humans, there are many other animals that have self-awareness — that is, they are conscious of themselves. It is also clear that most animals that we exploit for food, clothing and entertainment are capable of feeling physical pain and psychological stress. This can manifest itself in stereotypical behaviour or other forms such as cannibalism. To prevent animals from being subjected to unnecessary pain, many organisations advocate that animals be granted basic existential rights. These include the right to life, legal personality, physical and mental integrity, and the absolute prohibition of cruel treatment and torture. Due to their similar perception of life and way of feeling, animals should therefore be given both moral and legal consideration. read more

Speciesism

What is speciesism you may ask. The term speciesism has various definitions. It was originally coined by Richard D. Ryder, who defined it as "a prejudice or attitude of bias in favour of the interests of members of one's own species and against those of members of other species". The term became widely known through the book Animal Liberation by Australian philosopher Peter Singer. read more
